Project: Ensemble Techniques - Term Deposit Subscription Prediction¶

The dataset contains data on 5000 customers. The data include customer demographic information (age, income, etc.), the customer's relationship with the bank (mortgage, securities account, etc.), and the customer response to the last personal loan campaign (Personal Loan). Among these 5000 customers, only 480 (= 9.6%) accepted the personal loan that was offered to them in the earlier campaign.

Goal¶

Using the data collected from existing customers, build a model that will help the marketing team identify potential customers who are relatively more likely to subscribe term deposit and thus increase their hit ratio.

Conclusions from Univariate Analysis¶

  • Categorical variables: Distribution of categorical variables are reasonable and at this point there is no need to remove any categorical variables from the analysis. Also there is no need to convert any categorical variables except education into numbers as there is not much logical order for the categorical variables.
  • There is lot of skewness in the continuous variables previous, pdays, campaign and day
  • Education will be assigned numerical values
  • There is no missing data. But there are unknown values. It is difficult to assign an arbitrary value for the unknown values.
  • All non-numeric object type columns are converted to categorical columns
  • Negative values for account balance will be updated to 0
  • Account balance is highly skewed
  • There are outliers for duration, campaign
  • pdays is highly skewed and all values are -1 up to 75 percentiles
  • Previous is highly skewed with 0 till 75 percentiles
  • Based on the above assumptions, after univariate analysis there is no need to drop any columns or assign any default values (except for account balance) to any missing or unknown values. The data has only 12 percent conversion rate. So, there is no need to drop any columns at this point.

Conclusions from Univariate Analysis¶

  • 11.7% of the population subscribed to the term deposit. Looking at the countplot and cross tab for categorical variables we can make few concusions.
  1. Retired people and students are more likely to take term deposit
  2. People with existing defaults or loans are less likely to take the term depsoit
  3. Calls made in March, September and October had high hit ratio. But this seems to be outliers since less calls were made in these months.
  4. Last contact duration has a positive impact in hit ratio.
  5. Housing contact and pdaay are having reasonable impact on target outcome
